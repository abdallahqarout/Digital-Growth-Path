Q1

Why Tech Skills At All?

A great number of motivated people have great hopes and dreams and are aspiring to develop their skillset — however, they are often hindered by something very simple: tech skills. In today's technological era, it is nearly impossible to manage anything without having at least basic tech skills, which have become essential across daily life and the greatest majority of careers.

Some statistics show that 92% of jobs generally require tech skills, but only 67% of employees actually have the tech skills required for the job:

92% of Jobs Require Tech Skills

It is not difficult to see how the other 33% are at a severe disadvantage. Since our dependency on technology grows as time passes, employees with little to no tech skills may not be competent enough to manage any modern job. This turns basic tech skills from an optional hobby into an essential requirement before one can begin a career path.

Q2

Are Tech Skills Difficult to Learn?

If you think about tech skills as writing and reading endless lines of complex code, of course it will seem difficult — but that is a highly advanced level, not accessible to people who haven't yet developed basic tech skills. Such complexity isn't required in any non-tech career path.

On the other hand, the basic and most essential tech skills are neither difficult nor intimidating. They are accessible and can be learnt by everyone willing to put some time into learning. The right roadmap makes all the difference.
